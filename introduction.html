  • Professional Background:
    • I've worked in restaurants, a hotel, and retail, but for the last 6ish years, I've worked at a Managed Services Provider (MSP) based in South Charlotte. We provide network and security management and monitoring to a pretty wide array of corporations and handle a variety of technologies. I started as an entry level technician (lots of helping food service/retail employees find their routers and walking them through layer 1 troubleshooting) and worked my way up through the NOC (handling issues with more complex functions and networks), then out of the NOC. There, I've been a process manager, a project manager, a service manager, and I've been the director of Service Management for a little over 2 years now.

At Queens, I became drawn to their Psychology program (their professors are A++) and ended up switching my minor (Psychology) to my major and graduating in 2015 with a BA in Psychology and a minor in Human Biology. I'd always intended to return to school for nursing, but the costs (monetary and otherwise) of living and working have gotten in the way as they stereotypically do. I started fiddling around with learning various elements of programming years ago and found a way to kinda sorta work that sort of activity into part of my job. I recently realized how much I enjoy the process and seeing the finished (is anything ever finished, though?) product come to life. That and seeing the worklife my SO has (he's a software architect) has compelled me to try to break into the field, so here I am!
